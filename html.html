A tribute to

Colonel Birger Eriksen

The man who stopped Blücher

Colonel Birger Kristian Eriksen was an Norwegian officer, best known as the commanding officer for the soldiers that sank the German warship Blücher on its way to Oslo 9.April 1940. This is his story:

    Short timeline:

  • 1875 17.11.1875, Born in Lofoten, Nordland
  • 1896 Graduated Krigskolen, started his military career
  • 1915 Commander for Tønsberg fortress
  • 1918 Commander for Agdenes fortress as Colonel Lieutenant
  • 1933 Commander at Oscarborg fortress as Colonel
  • 1940 Sinking of Blücher
  • 1940 Retierd after the German ocucpation
  • 1945 Awarded "Krigskorset med sverd" for his efforts 9.April 1940
  • 1958 16.06.1958, Died in Oslo

9.April 1940, The night Eriksen changed the Norwegian history

Up until this date, the Norwegian government had been extremly naive as Norway was "neutral and had nothing to do with the European warfare." Even after reports of a large fleet of warships heading north from Germany, the government gave no order to prepare the fortresses for an attack. Eriksen however understood that this was a large scale invasion of Norway and gave his orders to prepare all cannons and torpedo batteries.
Sure enough, at 04:21 that night a large convoy off darkend ships passed Oscarsborg, heading for Oslo.Eriksen defied all his orders to only shoot warning shoots and fired directly at the first ship, an unexpected move for the Germans that exspected no resistance. The shoots sank Blücher and forced the rest off the convoy to rescue surviviors and go ashore to take the Oscarborg with infatery. They also launched a massive bombing raid against the fortress. Two hours later 850 troops where ashore and Eriksen surrenderd his fortress later that day.
The important part off these actions is that the German timetable got delayed by almost 24 hours, giving the government and the king time to escape from Oslo, and later to England where they lead the resistance. It also gave the bank time to evacuate all the gold reserves to a safe location, hiding it from the Germans.





Awarded

After the war, people that had made considerable efforts during the war got awarded. Eriksen was among them and recieved the higest decoration an offiser could get. "Krigskorset med sverd", directly translated to "War crusade with sword" He also got the French award "Croix de Guerre" and became an officer of the "French Honorary legion"
He`s also rememberd for these short and non-mistakable quotes:


"Warhero or warcriminal, fire!"

"Torpedo the ship."
